# Atopic Dermatitis — Recent Drugs & Clinical Trials

## Overview

The atopic dermatitis (AD) therapeutic landscape has undergone significant transformation in 2024-2026, with the approval and late-stage development of multiple novel biologic and small-molecule therapies targeting distinct inflammatory pathways. Beyond the established IL-4Rα inhibitor dupilumab, the field now offers IL-13 inhibitors (lebrikizumab, cendakimab), IL-31 receptor antagonists (nemolizumab), JAK inhibitors (upadacitinib, abrocitinib, baricitinib), OX40/OX40L pathway inhibitors (rocatinlimab, amlitelimab), and topical agents (ruxolitinib cream, roflumilast cream, tapinarof). This expansion has improved patient choice and enabled combination therapy strategies. Head-to-head and real-world comparative data are increasingly available, though black-box warnings for JAK inhibitors remain a regulatory concern regarding cardiovascular and thrombotic events in older patients and those with risk factors.

## Recent Approvals (2024-2026)

### Lebrikizumab (Ebglyss)
- **Approval Date:** September 2024
- **Indication:** Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis in patients 12 years of age and older
- **Mechanism:** IgG4 monoclonal antibody targeting IL-13; blocks IL-13 signaling via IL-4Rα/IL-13α1 receptor complex
- **Administration:** Subcutaneous injection (prefilled syringe or autoinjector)
- **Adverse Events:** Conjunctivitis, injection site reactions, herpes zoster
- **Source:** [FDA Approval of EBGLYSS (lebrikizumab-lbkz)](https://journals.lww.com/annals-of-medicine-and-surgery/fulltext/2025/10000/fda_approval_of_ebglyss___lebrikizumab_lbkz__to.1.aspx)

### Nemolizumab (Nemluvio)
- **Approval Dates:** August 2024 (prurigo nodularis); December 2024 (atopic dermatitis)
- **Indication:** Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis in patients 12 years of age and older
- **Mechanism:** Humanized IgG2 monoclonal antibody; selectively binds IL-31RA to inhibit IL-31 signaling
- **Adverse Events:** Headache (6% vs 3% placebo), atopic dermatitis exacerbation (4% vs 0.5%), nummular eczema (3% vs 0%)
- **Clinical Significance:** First IL-31 inhibitor biologic approved for AD
- **Source:** [Nemolizumab FDA Approval](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/nemolizumab-granted-fda-approval-for-moderate-to-severe-atopic-dermatitis)

### Ruxolitinib Cream (Opzelura) — Pediatric Expansion
- **Approval Date:** September 2025 (supplemental approval)
- **Indication:** Mild-to-moderate atopic dermatitis in non-immunocompromised patients 2 years and older
- **Previous Approval:** 2021 (ages 12+)
- **Mechanism:** Topical JAK inhibitor
- **Trial Basis:** Phase 3 TRuE-AD3 trial (ages 2-11 years)
- **Efficacy:** Significantly more patients achieved IGA treatment success vs vehicle control; EASI-75 secondary endpoint also met
- **Safety:** No new safety signals; no serious infections, major adverse cardiovascular events, malignancies, or thromboses in 8-week vehicle-controlled period
- **Source:** [Ruxolitinib Cream Pediatric Approval](https://www.ajmc.com/view/fda-approves-ruxolitinib-cream-for-patients-as-young-as-2-years-with-atopic-dermatitis)

### Roflumilast Cream (Zoryve)
- **Approval Dates:** July 2024 (0.15% for ages 6+); 2024 (0.05% for ages 2-5)
- **Indication:** Mild-to-moderate atopic dermatitis
- **Mechanism:** PDE-4 inhibitor; steroid-free topical cream for long-term disease control
- **Trial Basis:** INTEGUMENT-1 and INTEGUMENT-2 Phase 3 trials
- **Efficacy (INTEGUMENT-1):** 32.0% ZORYVE vs 15.2% vehicle achieved IGA Success; (INTEGUMENT-2): 28.9% vs 12.0%
- **Safety:** Rapid disease clearance with significant itch reduction; minimal systemic exposure
- **Source:** [Roflumilast (ZORYVE) FDA Approval](https://www.arcutis.com/fda-approves-arcutis-zoryve-roflumilast-cream-0-15-for-the-treatment-of-atopic-dermatitis-in-adults-and-children-down-to-6-years-of-age/)

### Tapinarof Cream (VTAMA)
- **Approval Date:** December 16, 2024
- **Indication:** Atopic dermatitis in adults and children 2 years and older
- **Mechanism:** Aryl hydrocarbon receptor agonist; topical, non-steroidal
- **Trial Basis:** ADORING 1 and ADORING 2 Phase 3 trials
- **Efficacy:** Up to 46% achieved treatment success at Week 8 vs 18% vehicle; itch improvement reported as early as 24 hours
- **Previous Approval:** May 2022 for plaque psoriasis (first non-steroidal topical novel entity for psoriasis in >25 years)
- **Source:** [VTAMA (tapinarof) FDA Approval for Atopic Dermatitis](https://www.organon.com/news/fda-approves-vtama-tapinarof-cream-1-for-the-treatment-of-atopic-dermatitis-in-adults-and-children-2-years-of-age-and-older/)

### Dupilumab & Tralokinumab — Pediatric Label Expansions (Prior to 2024)
- **Dupilumab:** Approved for infants 6 months and older; expanded to children 6-11 years, adolescents 12-17 years; now approved from 6 months—broadest pediatric coverage among biologics
- **Tralokinumab:** December 2023 expansion for ages 12-17 years (IL-13 inhibitor mechanism)
- **Source:** [Dupilumab & Tralokinumab Pediatric Label Updates](https://www.dermatologyadvisor.com/cch/atopic-dermatitis-aad-2025-biologics-lebrikizumab-dupilumab-tralokinumab/)

## Late-Stage Pipeline & Key Phase 3 Readouts

### Rocatinlimab (AMG 451 / KHK4083) — OX40 Pathway Inhibitor
- **Sponsor:** Amgen and Kyowa Kirin
- **Mechanism of Action:** T-cell rebalancing agent targeting OX40 receptor; blocks pathogenic T cell activation and reduces T cell numbers
- **Phase 3 Program:** ROCKET (eight global trials in adults and adolescents with moderate-to-severe AD)
- **ROCKET-IGNITE Trial (24 weeks):**
  - **Population:** 769 adults with moderate-to-severe AD
  - **Primary Endpoints Met:** Both rocatinlimab dose strengths vs placebo achieved statistical significance
  - **Efficacy (Higher Dose):** 42.3% achieved EASI-75 response
  - **Clinical Benefit:** Patients ~3x more likely to achieve significant eczema severity improvement vs placebo
  - **Readout:** 2025
- **ROCKET-HORIZON Trial (24 weeks):**
  - **Population:** ~1,500 patients followed across IGNITE and HORIZON combined
  - **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized across 19 countries
  - **Results:** Robust and lasting benefits demonstrated
- **ASCEND Long-Term Extension (1 year):**
  - **Outcome:** Majority of patients receiving Q4W or Q8W dosing reported continued therapeutic benefit
  - **Measures:** Improvement in skin clearance, itch, disease extent, and severity
  - **Readout:** September 2025
- **Source:** [Rocatinlimab Phase 3 ROCKET Program Overview](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1750743X.2025.2464528); [Amgen IGNITE Results](https://www.amgen.com/newsroom/press-releases/2025/03/amgen-and-kyowa-kirin-provide-top-line-results-from-rocatinlimab-phase-3-ignite-study-in-adults-with-moderate-to-severe-atopic-dermatitis); [Lancet Publication ROCKET-IGNITE/HORIZON](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(25)01865-3/abstract)

### Amlitelimab (SAR440340) — OX40L Inhibitor
- **Sponsor:** Sanofi
- **Mechanism of Action:** Fully human, non-T cell depleting monoclonal antibody targeting OX40 ligand (OX40L)
- **Phase 3 Program:** OCEANA (multiple trials: COAST 1, COAST 2, SHORE, AQUA, ESTUARY)
- **COAST 1 Trial (24 weeks):**
  - **Dosing:** Q4W vs Q12W dosing schedules
  - **Population:** Adults and adolescents 12+ with moderate-to-severe AD
  - **Primary Endpoint (vIGA-AD 0/1):**
    - Q4W: 21.1% vs Placebo: 9.2%
    - Q12W: 22.5% vs Placebo: 9.2%
  - **EASI-75 Efficacy:**
    - Q4W: 35.9% vs Placebo: 19.1%
    - Q12W: 39.1% vs Placebo: 19.1%
  - **Clinical Significance:** Possible 4x/year dosing (Q12W arm)
- **COAST 2 Trial Results (2026 AAD presentation):**
  - **vIGA-AD 0/1:**
    - Q4W: 25.3% vs Placebo: 14.8%
    - Q12W: 25.7% vs Placebo: 14.8%
- **SHORE Trial (Combination with Topical Corticosteroids):**
  - **vIGA-AD 0/1:**
    - Q4W: 28.7% vs Placebo: 16.8%
    - Q12W: 32.3% vs Placebo: 16.8%
- **Status:** Met all primary and key secondary endpoints; potentially first atopic dermatitis treatment with only 4x/year dosing option
- **Source:** [Sanofi Amlitelimab COAST 1 Results](https://www.sanofi.com/en/media-room/press-releases/2025/2025-09-04-05-00-00-3144170); [Dermatology Times COAST Results Summary](https://www.dermatologytimes.com/view/sanofi-s-ox40l-inhibitor-amlitelimab-shows-positive-results-in-phase-3-ad-trial)

### Cendakimab (IL-13 Inhibitor)
- **Sponsor:** Bristol Myers Squibb (discontinued AD development February 2023)
- **Mechanism of Action:** Selective IL-13 inhibitor targeting IL-13Rα1 and IL-13Rα2
- **Phase 2 Results (2024 publication):**
  - **Primary Endpoint:** Met for 720 mg once weekly vs placebo at Week 16 (significant EASI score reduction)
  - **Dose:** 720 mg every 2 weeks missed statistical significance
  - **Safety:** Generally safe and well-tolerated
- **Development Status:** No Phase 3 trials ongoing for atopic dermatitis; development discontinued in AD to pursue other IL-13 inhibitors (lebrikizumab, etc.). Currently being examined for eosinophilic esophagitis and gastroenteritis.
- **Source:** [Cendakimab Phase 2 JAMA Dermatology Trial](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamadermatology/fullarticle/2821285)

### Tezepelumab (Anti-TSLP) — Limited AD Development
- **Mechanism of Action:** Anti-thymic stromal lymphopoietin monoclonal antibody
- **Phase 2a Trial (Completed, No Phase 3 for AD):**
  - **Population:** 113 patients with moderate-to-severe AD
  - **Design:** Randomized, double-blinded, placebo-controlled
  - **Dose & Schedule:** 280 mg subcutaneous every 2 weeks plus topical corticosteroids
  - **Efficacy at Week 12:** EASI-50: 64.7% tezepelumab vs 48.2% placebo (p=0.091, NOT statistically significant)
  - **Key Finding:** No statistically significant improvements in EASI-50, EASI-75, or pruritus NRS
- **Current Development:** Ongoing trials in CRSwNP, COPD, and chronic spontaneous urticaria, but NOT atopic dermatitis
- **Source:** [Tezepelumab Phase 2a AD Trial PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30550828/)

## Safety & Comparative Data

### JAK Inhibitors — Black-Box Warnings & Long-Term Safety

#### FDA Black-Box Warnings (Baricitinib, Upadacitinib, Abrocitinib)
- **Regulatory Basis:** Following safety review of tofacitinib, FDA determined increased risks of serious adverse events
- **Documented Risks:**
  - Serious heart-related events (myocardial infarction, stroke)
  - Blood clots (venous thromboembolism)
  - Cancer
  - Death
- **Clinical Monitoring Recommendations:** Physicians advised to weigh risks/benefits, particularly in:
  - Current or past smokers
  - Patients with cardiovascular risk factors
  - Those with known malignancy
  - Older patients
- **Note:** Baricitinib and upadacitinib have not undergone the same large dedicated safety trial (ORAL Surveillance) as tofacitinib, so comparative risk profiles remain incompletely defined

#### Long-Term Safety Data (2024-2025)

**Baricitinib (Pediatric Extension to 3.6 Years):**
- **Sustained efficacy** with **no new safety signals** identified
- Extended safety profile demonstrates durability in children
- Source: [Baricitinib 3.6-Year Extended Safety](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09546634.2024.2411834)

**Upadacitinib & Abrocitinib (Real-World Data 2025):**
- **Dose-dependent effectiveness** and patient-reported outcomes over 36 weeks
- Multicenter real-world cohort studies confirm safety in older adults (65+ years)
- JAK1 inhibitors demonstrate manageable safety in long-term monitoring
- Source: [Real-World JAK1 Safety in Older Adults](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-32163-y)

### Biologic vs JAK Inhibitor Comparative Real-World Data (2025)

#### Real-World Effectiveness Study (Southern Spain, 202 Patients)
- **Population:** 134 treated with biologics (dupilumab, tralokinumab, lebrikizumab, nemolizumab), 68 with JAK inhibitors
- **Study Period:** Patients initiated between 2019-2024
- **Dermatologic Adverse Events:**
  - Facial erythema and xerosis: significantly more frequent in biologic group
  - Acne: significantly more prevalent in JAK inhibitor group
- **Ophthalmologic Complications:**
  - Dry eye and pruritus: significantly more common in biologic group
- **Mechanistic Differences:**
  - **Biologics:** Target specific cytokines (IL-4Rα, IL-13, IL-31), durable type 2 control
  - **JAK inhibitors:** Broad JAK-STAT pathway inhibition, rapid onset (days to weeks)
- **Clinical Implication:** Choice between classes depends on patient factors, adverse event tolerance, and disease severity
- Source: [Comparative Real-World Effectiveness Southern Spain](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/medicine/articles/10.3389/fmed.2025.1658843/full)

### Combination Therapy — JAK + Biologic (2024 Case Series)

**Dupilumab + JAK Inhibitor in Refractory Disease:**
- **Study:** 16 weeks, 16 patients (age 5-73) with refractory moderate-to-severe AD
- **Regimens:**
  - Dupilumab + Baricitinib 2 mg (n=7)
  - Dupilumab + Abrocitinib 100 mg (n=5)
  - Dupilumab + Upadacitinib 15 mg (n=4)
- **Efficacy Outcomes at 16 weeks:**
  - EASI-50: 100% of patients
  - EASI-75: 87.5%
  - EASI-90: 37.5%
  - Median pruritus score: 8.0 → 1.0
  - Quality-of-life measures: marked reductions
- **Comparative Benefit:** Upadacitinib showed slightly greater EASI reduction vs abrocitinib
- **Safety:** Well-tolerated; minimal adverse events (one patient mild ocular symptoms, self-resolved); no serious lab abnormalities; no treatment discontinuations
- **Clinical Implication:** Dual mechanism therapy offers synergistic benefit in treatment-refractory cases with acceptable safety
- Source: [Dupilumab + JAK Inhibitor Combination](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1155/dth/9515524)

### JAK Inhibitor Head-to-Head Efficacy (Pediatric Network Meta-Analysis 2025)

- **Comparison:** Upadacitinib vs abrocitinib vs baricitinib vs dupilumab in pediatric AD
- **Key Finding:** Upadacitinib 30 mg demonstrated highest efficacy
  - 62% more patients achieved clinical improvement vs placebo
  - vs Abrocitinib 100 mg: 40% improvement
  - vs Abrocitinib 200 mg: 40% improvement
- **Implication:** Among JAK inhibitors, upadacitinib 30 mg may offer superior pediatric outcomes
- Source: [JAK Inhibitor Pediatric Network Meta-Analysis](https://www.emjreviews.com/dermatology/congress-review/advances-in-jak-inhibitors-for-atopic-dermatitis-insights-from-the-2025-aad-conference-j240125/)

## Open Questions

- **Long-term durability of OX40/OX40L inhibitors:** Rocatinlimab and amlitelimab show 24-week and 1-year data; extended 2-3 year safety and efficacy data needed
- **Optimal dosing strategies:** Amlitelimab's Q12W (4x/year) arm; rocatinlimab's Q4W vs Q8W; comparative adherence and breakthrough rates unknown
- **Comparative efficacy gaps:** No head-to-head trials directly comparing rocatinlimab, amlitelimab, and lebrikizumab; ranking uncertain
- **Sequential vs combination therapy:** Emerging combination data (dupilumab + JAK); optimal sequencing and dual biologic approaches unclear
- **Risk stratification for JAK inhibitors:** Black-box warnings apply to all JAK inhibitors, but comparative thrombotic/cardiovascular risk among upadacitinib, abrocitinib, baricitinib not well defined
- **Pediatric data gaps:** Most newer biologics (rocatinlimab, amlitelimab, cendakimab) lack pediatric Phase 3 data; expansion trials ongoing
- **Anti-TSLP in AD:** Why tezepelumab failed Phase 2 in AD while succeeding in asthma/CRSwNP remains mechanistically unclear
- **Cost-effectiveness and real-world utilization:** Limited health economic data comparing topical JAK, PDE-4, AhR agonists vs injectable biologics
- **Biomarker-driven patient selection:** No validated biomarkers yet to predict responders to IL-4Rα, IL-13, IL-31, or OX40 pathways
- **Systemic absorption of topical agents:** Long-term safety of topical ruxolitinib, roflumilast, and tapinarof in infants and young children with extensive skin barrier dysfunction
